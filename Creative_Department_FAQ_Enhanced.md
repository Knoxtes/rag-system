### Q: What is the average timeline to develop a website?

**Answer**: Website development timelines vary significantly based on project complexity and scope. There is no universal "average" timeline because each project has unique variables that affect the schedule, including:

- **Project Scope**: Number of pages, custom functionality requirements, e-commerce integration, content management needs
- **Design Complexity**: Custom design vs. template-based, number of unique page layouts, interactive elements
- **Content Availability**: The most common factor affecting timeline is timely receipt of content (text, images, videos) from the client
- **Revision Cycles**: Number of feedback rounds and client approval stages
- **Third-party Integrations**: API connections, payment gateways, CRM systems, analytics setup

**Important**: We cannot provide an accurate timeline estimate without first scoping the project through a Client Needs Assessment (CNA). Once we understand the full scope, we can provide a realistic project timeline with milestone dates.

**Best Practice**: Set client expectations early that their responsiveness in providing content and feedback directly impacts the project completion date.

### Q: What's the difference between a landing page and a full website?

**Answer**: 

**Landing Page**:
- A single, focused web page designed for a specific marketing campaign or conversion goal
- Typically contains targeted content with a single call-to-action (CTA)
- Used for lead generation, product launches, event registration, or promotional campaigns
- Limited navigation to keep visitors focused on the primary conversion goal
- Faster to develop and deploy (typically 1-2 weeks)
- Lower cost investment compared to full website development

**Full Website**:
- Multi-page digital presence with comprehensive information architecture
- Includes homepage, about pages, services/products pages, contact forms, blog, etc.
- Multiple navigation paths and user journeys
- Serves as the primary online hub for business information, branding, and customer engagement
- Longer development timeline (typically 4-12+ weeks depending on scope)
- Higher investment but provides complete digital presence

**Use Case Recommendations**:
- Choose a landing page for: time-sensitive campaigns, specific product launches, lead generation initiatives, A/B testing
- Choose a full website for: establishing comprehensive online presence, ongoing content marketing, multiple service offerings, brand authority building

## Logo Design Services

### Q: How long does it take to create a logo?

**Answer**: Logo design is a collaborative, multi-phase process that balances creative excellence with timely delivery. Our standard timeline is structured as follows:

**Standard Timeline**:
- **Initial Concepts**: First round of logo concepts is typically finalized and ready for presentation **2 weeks after the onboarding call**
- **Total Project Duration**: 3-6 weeks depending on revision cycles and client responsiveness

**Process Breakdown**:

1. **Discovery & Onboarding** (Week 1):
   - Client onboarding call to understand brand values, target audience, and design preferences
   - Competitive analysis and market research
   - Mood board development and style direction

2. **Concept Development** (Week 2):
   - Initial design concepts created (typically 3-5 distinct directions)
   - Internal design review and refinement
   - Preparation of presentation materials

3. **First Presentation** (End of Week 2):
   - Present initial concepts to client
   - Gather detailed feedback on each direction

4. **Revision Rounds** (Weeks 3-4+):
   - Refine selected concept(s) based on client feedback
   - Number of revision rounds varies by project complexity and client decisiveness
   - Additional rounds beyond the standard 2-3 may incur additional fees

5. **Finalization** (Final Week):
   - Final approval and sign-off
   - Production of all file formats
   - Delivery of complete logo package

**Timeline Variables**:
- **Client Responsiveness**: Faster feedback = shorter timeline
- **Revision Complexity**: Simple tweaks vs. major design pivots
- **Stakeholder Alignment**: Single decision-maker vs. committee approval
- **Project Complexity**: Simple wordmark vs. complex illustrative mark

**Best Practices**:
- Set clear expectations about feedback turnaround times during onboarding
- Encourage consolidated feedback (all stakeholders review together)
- Define approval process and decision-makers upfront
- Consider express options for time-sensitive projects (additional fees apply)

### Q: What is the difference between a logo and an illustration?

**Answer**: While both logos and illustrations are visual design elements, they serve distinctly different purposes and are created with different considerations in mind.

**LOGO**:

**Purpose**: A logo is a symbol of brand identity designed for consistent, widespread use across all marketing materials and platforms.

**Characteristics**:
- **Simplicity**: Clean, easily recognizable design that works at any size
- **Versatility**: Must function in full color, black & white, single color, and reverse applications
- **Scalability**: Looks sharp from business card size (Â½ inch) to billboard size (20+ feet)
- **Timelessness**: Designed to remain relevant for years or decades
- **Memorability**: Instantly recognizable and distinctively represents the brand
- **File Formats**: Delivered in vector format (AI, EPS, SVG) for infinite scalability

**Common Uses**:
- Business cards, letterhead, email signatures
- Website headers and favicons
- Social media profiles
- Signage and vehicle wraps
- Product packaging and labels
- Advertisements and marketing materials

**Pricing**: Logo design is typically billed as a comprehensive branding project ($2,000-$15,000+) including multiple concepts, revisions, and complete file packages.

---

**ILLUSTRATION**:

**Purpose**: An illustration is a custom artwork created for a specific application, campaign, or piece of content.

**Characteristics**:
- **Complexity**: Can be highly detailed and intricate
- **Context-Specific**: Designed for particular use case or medium
- **Artistic Expression**: More creative freedom and stylistic variation
- **Narrative**: Often tells a story or conveys a specific message
- **Medium-Dependent**: May be optimized for print, web, or specific size requirements
- **File Formats**: Delivered in appropriate format for intended use (may be raster or vector)

**Common Uses**:
- Magazine or book illustrations
- Infographics and data visualizations
- Marketing campaign artwork
- Website hero images or backgrounds
- Social media campaign graphics
- Editorial content
- Decorative elements for specific projects

**Pricing**: Illustrations are typically billed per project or per piece ($500-$5,000+) based on complexity, usage rights, and timeline.

---

**Key Distinction**:
- A **logo** is your brand's visual identity used everywhere, forever
- An **illustration** is custom artwork for a specific purpose or campaign

**Can They Overlap?**
Sometimes logos incorporate illustrative elements (called "illustrative marks"), but they must still maintain the core principles of logo design: simplicity, scalability, and versatility.

**Example**: 
- The Nike swoosh is a **logo** - simple, scalable, used everywhere
- A custom drawing of an athlete wearing Nike shoes for an ad campaign is an **illustration** - detailed, context-specific, created for that particular campaign

### Q: Can I use a social media graphic as a badge on the app/website?

**Answer**: No. Social media graphics and app/website badges are designed for fundamentally different technical requirements and user experiences. They are not interchangeable.

**Why Social Media Graphics Don't Work as Badges**:

**Size Constraints**:
- **Badges** display at very small sizes (often 50-150 pixels)
- **Social graphics** are designed for larger viewing (1080px+)
- Text and details that are clear in social posts become illegible when shrunk to badge size

**Design Complexity**:
- **Badges** must be simple, clean, and instantly recognizable at thumbnail size
- **Social graphics** can include detailed information, multiple text elements, and complex designs
- Background patterns and gradients that work for social become muddy in small badge format

**Technical Requirements**:
- **Badges** require transparent backgrounds (PNG format)
- **Badges** need to work on both light and dark backgrounds
- **Badges** must be optimized for retina/high-DPI displays
- **Badges** have specific aspect ratios (often square or specific rectangles)

**Visual Hierarchy**:
- **Badges** prioritize icon/symbol over text
- **Badges** use minimal or no text (logo/symbol recognition)
- **Social graphics** are designed to be read and include promotional copy

**Examples of Badge-Appropriate Designs**:
- Station logo in circular format
- Simple icon representing a show or feature
- Minimalist symbol with high contrast
- Clean, recognizable shapes and colors

**How to Request Proper Badges**:

1. **Submit Separate Request**: Create a new Trello card specifically for badge creation
2. **Specify Usage**: Clearly indicate this is for app/website badge use
3. **Provide Requirements**: Include exact dimensions and technical specifications
4. **Reference Examples**: Link to similar badges already in use if available
5. **Turnaround**: Allow 3-5 business days for custom badge design

**Best Practice**: Always request badges as separate design projects rather than trying to repurpose existing marketing graphics. The design team will create optimized versions specifically for small-format display.

### Q: Trello isn't loading. Can you help?

**Answer**: If Trello is experiencing loading issues or technical difficulties, this is typically due to platform-level issues outside of our direct control.

**Troubleshooting Steps**:

1. **Check Platform Status**: 
   - Visit **trello.status.atlassian.com** to check if Atlassian (Trello's parent company) is reporting any known issues or outages
   - This status page shows real-time information about service disruptions, scheduled maintenance, or technical incidents

2. **Try Basic Troubleshooting**:
   - **Refresh the page** (Ctrl+R or Cmd+R)
   - **Clear browser cache** and cookies for Trello
   - **Try a different browser** (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge)
   - **Check your internet connection**
   - **Try the Trello mobile app** as an alternative
   - **Disable browser extensions** that might interfere with Trello

3. **Report the Issue**:
   - If the status page shows no known issues but you're still experiencing problems, report it through the Atlassian support page
   - Provide specific details: error messages, browser version, what you were trying to do when the issue occurred

4. **Alternative Workflows**:
   - While Trello is down, document urgent requests via email to the appropriate department
   - Once Trello is restored, add those requests to the proper boards for tracking
   - Check with team members to see if others are experiencing the same issue (helps determine if it's a widespread outage or a local issue)

**Resource**: [Trello Status Page](https://trello.status.atlassian.com/)

**Internal IT Support**: If you suspect the issue is specific to your account or device (not a platform-wide issue), contact internal IT support for additional troubleshooting assistance.

2. **Email Address**: The email associated with their Trello account
3. **Reason for Removal**: 
   - Employee departure/termination
   - Department transfer (no longer needs access)
   - End of contractor/freelancer engagement
   - Role change requiring different access
4. **Urgency Level**: 
   - **Standard** (processed within 1 business day)
   - **Immediate** (terminations requiring same-day action for security)
5. **Scope of Removal**:
   - Remove from entire workspace (all boards)
   - Remove from specific boards only (list which boards)

**Processing Time**: 
- **Standard removals**: Within 1 business day
- **Urgent removals** (security concerns): Same day, often within hours

**Security Note**: For employee terminations or security-sensitive situations, clearly mark your email as "URGENT" and consider following up with a phone call to ensure prompt action.

**Partial Access Changes**: If someone needs access adjusted (moved between boards) rather than complete removal, specify the desired final state in your request.

**Compliance**: User access removal requests are logged for security compliance and audit purposes.
